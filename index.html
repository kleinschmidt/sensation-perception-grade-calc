Sensation and perception grade calculator

The final exam will have two parts. The first part will have 30 questions and cover material since the second midterm (classes 19-24). The second part will have 60 questions and is cumulative. That means there are four assessments from this class: the two midterms, the non-cumulative portion of the final, and the cumulative portion. Your lowest score of the four will be dropped (using the percentage correct to determine which is lowest).

This means that you can skip one part of the final without penalty. If you don't take one part of the final, a grade of zero will be entered which will be dropped from your final grade.

Because the four assessments have different numbers of questions, they have different weights in the calculation of the final grade. This might be confusing so I've created a small calculator to help you see what would happen with different scores on the final.

How to use the calculator

Enter your scores on the first two midterms, and adjust the anticipated scores for the two parts of the final (non-cumulative covers classes 19-24; cumulative covers entire semester).

For all exams, put in the number of questions correct (the score listed on Canvas, not the percentage.

Put a 0 in to simulate skipping one part of the final, or leave it blank
